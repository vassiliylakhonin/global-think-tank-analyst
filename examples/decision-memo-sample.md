# Sample Decision Memo

## Query
`think-tank --risk Export-control escalation in semiconductors (2026-2028)`

## Executive summary
Escalation risk is **medium-high** over 24 months. The most plausible pathway is incremental restrictions plus licensing friction, not immediate blanket bans.

## Core thesis
The dominant risk is not a single policy shock, but a chain of narrower controls that compound into delivery delays, compliance cost growth, and partner reconfiguration.

## Assumptions
1. Major blocs keep strategic competition below open conflict.
2. Enforcement tightens unevenly by jurisdiction.
3. Firms reprioritize resilience over pure cost optimization.

## Alternative hypothesis
Controls stall due to domestic inflation pressure and business lobbying, creating a temporary de-escalation window.

## Risk matrix
- Licensing bottlenecks: Likelihood high, Impact medium-high
- Supplier concentration shock: Likelihood medium, Impact high
- Compliance overreach penalties: Likelihood medium, Impact medium-high

## Go / No-Go triggers
- **Go resilience program** if lead times worsen by >20% in 2 consecutive quarters.
- **No-Go major capex shift** unless 2+ jurisdictions announce synchronized hardening.

## 1-2 week validation plan
- Track regulator bulletins (US/EU/JP/CN).
- Verify distributor lead-time movement across top components.
- Re-estimate downside exposure under a +15% compliance-cost scenario.

## Confidence
**Medium** (policy direction is clear; timing and intensity remain uncertain).
